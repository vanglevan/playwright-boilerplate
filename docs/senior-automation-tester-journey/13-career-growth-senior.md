# 13 — Career Growth (Senior → Staff/Principal)

> Senior is a plateau for many. The path to Staff/Principal requires intentional choices.
> This doc maps the 3-5 years after senior.

---

## 🎯 The career ladder (2026 reality)

### Typical tech levels

```
IC Track                          Manager Track
─────────                         ─────────
Junior (0-2 yrs)        ←→        (rarely manager)
Mid (2-5 yrs)           ←→        (rarely)
Senior (5-8 yrs)        ←→        Team Lead (5-10 yrs)
Staff (8-12 yrs)        ←→        Engineering Manager (8+ yrs)
Principal (10-15 yrs)   ←→        Director (10-15 yrs)
Distinguished (15+ yrs) ←→        VP (15+ yrs)
```

Automation testers can follow IC track (individual contributor) to Staff+ just like developers.

### Level definitions (general)

| Level         | Scope              | Impact             |
| ------------- | ------------------ | ------------------ |
| Junior        | Tasks              | Personal output    |
| Mid           | Features           | Team output        |
| Senior        | Projects / domain  | Team success       |
| Staff         | Teams / cross-team | Organization       |
| Principal     | Organization       | Company direction  |
| Distinguished | Industry           | Industry direction |

### QA-specific levels

Many companies merge "QA Senior" with "SDET" / "Test Engineer". In 2026:

- Some rename to "Quality Engineer" (broader)
- Some drop QA-specific titles for unified engineering ladder
- Specialization roles: "Performance Engineer", "Security Engineer"

## 🚀 The Senior → Staff transition

### Why it's hard

- Senior = technical mastery (learnable)
- Staff = leadership + technical (messier)

### What changes at Staff

#### 1. Impact scope

- Senior: your team's output
- Staff: multiple teams / org function

#### 2. Work nature

- Senior: 60% hands-on, 40% coordination
- Staff: 30% hands-on, 70% strategy + people

#### 3. Rewards

- Senior: solving hard problems
- Staff: enabling others to solve problems

#### 4. Failure mode

- Senior: code quality slips
- Staff: fail to multiply team

### Two senior → staff paths (Will Larson's framework)

#### The Tech Lead

- Owns tech direction for team(s)
- Still codes substantially
- Deep in 1-2 domains

#### The Architect

- Designs cross-team systems
- Less hands-on
- Influence via documents + meetings

#### The Solver

- Attacks hardest problems across org
- Roams where needed
- Trusted for high-stakes work

#### The Right Hand

- Partners with executive (VPE, CTO)
- Strategic multiplier
- Rare path

Pick 1-2 that resonate. Build toward.

### Signals of Staff-ready

Self-assess:

- [ ] Tech strategy for 1+ teams written
- [ ] Impact visible beyond immediate team
- [ ] Mentored 2+ people to next level
- [ ] Recognized expertise in 1 domain
- [ ] Can manage complex projects (not just tasks)
- [ ] Leadership team asks your opinion
- [ ] Cross-org initiatives led
- [ ] Published / presented externally

Need ~70% to be in conversation for Staff.

### Common stalls

#### Stall 1: "Hero senior"

Senior who does everything themselves. Never trusts team. Can't promote because team falls apart without them.
**Fix:** Delegate. Multiply team.

#### Stall 2: "Invisible senior"

Great work, team knows it. Leadership doesn't. No promo.
**Fix:** Self-promote thoughtfully. Visible wins.

#### Stall 3: "Technical only"

Deep expertise, weak communication. Influence limited.
**Fix:** Invest in soft skills (Doc 12).

#### Stall 4: "Manager aversion"

Resents spending time on "non-coding". But Staff needs it.
**Fix:** Accept role evolution, or stay senior happily.

## 🌳 Specialization tracks (at senior level)

### Track A: AI-Native Testing Engineer

**Focus:** Testing AI applications + using AI for testing.

**Skills:**

- LLM evaluation
- Prompt injection testing (security)
- Hallucination detection
- AI agent evaluation
- Multi-modal testing

**Growth path:**

- Senior → Staff AI QA → Principal AI Engineer (testing focus)
- Or → AI Testing Architect

**Demand 2026:** HIGH. Most companies need this, few qualified.

**Downside:** Field moves fast. Must continuously learn.

### Track B: Test Architect / Strategist

**Focus:** Design test strategies across teams/orgs.

**Skills:**

- Multi-team strategy
- Test infrastructure design
- Process + tooling decisions
- Cross-functional leadership

**Growth path:**

- Senior → Staff Test Architect → Principal Engineer (Quality)

**Demand:** Moderate. Fewer opportunities than AI but stable.

**Downside:** Less hands-on, more political.

### Track C: Performance Engineer

**Focus:** Load testing, optimization, capacity.

**Skills:**

- k6 / JMeter deep
- Distributed system performance
- Profiling (CPU, memory, I/O)
- Capacity planning

**Growth path:**

- Senior → Performance Architect → Principal Performance Engineer

**Demand:** Moderate, stable. SaaS + high-scale apps need.

**Downside:** Niche may limit options in smaller companies.

### Track D: Security/DevSecOps

**Focus:** Security testing + shift-left security.

**Skills:**

- OWASP Top 10 deep
- Pen testing basics
- SAST / DAST tools
- Cloud security
- Compliance (SOC2, ISO)

**Growth path:**

- Senior → DevSecOps Engineer → Security Architect

**Demand:** HIGH (growing). Especially FinTech, HealthTech.

**Downside:** Requires certifications often (OSCP, CISSP).

### Track E: Mobile Testing Specialist

**Focus:** iOS + Android native and cross-platform.

**Skills:**

- Appium deep
- Native test frameworks (Espresso, XCUITest)
- Device farms
- Mobile CI/CD
- App store release processes

**Growth path:**

- Senior → Mobile Test Lead → Principal Mobile Engineer

**Demand:** Mobile-first companies hiring. Less demand at web-only.

### Track F: Reliability / SRE

**Focus:** Production reliability, observability, chaos.

**Skills:**

- Observability (OTel, Datadog)
- Chaos engineering
- Incident response
- SLO/SLI/SLA design
- On-call experience

**Growth path:**

- Senior → SRE → Staff SRE

**Demand:** HIGH (especially at scale).

**Downside:** Often means on-call (pager).

### Track G: Management

**Focus:** Leading people, team, org.

**Skills:**

- 1:1s
- Career development for reports
- Hiring
- Strategy with execs
- Performance reviews

**Growth path:**

- Senior → Team Lead → Engineering Manager → Director

**Demand:** Stable.

**Downside:** Very different from IC work. Can't easily go back.

### How to pick

Questions to self-answer:

1. What gets me excited vs drains me?
2. What's my market saying (job listings, recruiter pings)?
3. What does my company need?
4. What's my company's pay for that track?
5. Do I want coding-heavy or coordination-heavy?

Try 2 tracks lightly in Month 4-6 (bootcamp's M4-M6) before committing.

Don't over-commit. Can pivot if misread.

## 💰 Compensation deep dive

### Components of total comp

1. **Base salary** — cash, predictable
2. **Bonus** — annual, variable
3. **Equity** — stocks/options (if startup/public)
4. **Benefits** — health, retirement, PTO, perks
5. **Signing bonus** — one-time
6. **Relocation** — one-time
7. **Equity refresher** — additional stock grants over time

Total comp = sum of all.

### Research tools

#### Vietnam

- [TopDev Salary Report](https://topdev.vn/) — annual
- [ITviec salary](https://itviec.com/salary)
- Company Glassdoor pages
- Direct network (ask peers, carefully)

#### Global / remote

- [Levels.fyi](https://www.levels.fyi/) — tech-heavy, accurate
- [Glassdoor](https://www.glassdoor.com/)
- [Blind](https://www.teamblind.com/) — anonymous
- [Payscale](https://www.payscale.com/)

### Negotiation essentials

#### Before offer

- Know your number (target, floor)
- Know market range (research)
- Know your leverage (competing offers, specialized skills)

#### During offer call

- Don't quote first — "What's the range for this role?"
- Delay: "Let me discuss with family" (even if not)
- Anchor high on first response

#### Counter-offer

- Practice out loud (weird but crucial)
- Stay warm: "I'm excited about the role. Based on my research + experience, I was expecting closer to $X. Is there flexibility?"
- Be specific: "Could we get to $X base + $Y signing + $Z equity?"
- Pause after asking — silence is your ally

#### What to negotiate

Everything is negotiable in theory:

- Base ★★★ easiest
- Signing bonus ★★
- Equity (range) ★★
- Equity refresher schedule ★
- Vacation days ★
- Start date ★
- Title ★ (sometimes)
- Remote flexibility ★★
- Learning budget ★

### When to negotiate

- Yes: New role / offer (highest leverage)
- Yes: Annual review (if solid performance)
- Maybe: Retention counter (if leaving)
- Rarely: Mid-year (less leverage)

### Common mistakes

- Accepting first offer (always negotiate)
- Disclosing current salary (illegal in some areas — check)
- Negotiating after accepting (usually too late)
- Being greedy (companies remember)

### Read

- [Patrick McKenzie — Salary Negotiation](https://www.kalzumeus.com/2012/01/23/salary-negotiation/) — **definitive guide**

## 🎯 Interview prep at Senior level

### Interview formats

#### 1. Coding interview (same as earlier levels, often)

Data structures, algorithms.

- LeetCode mediums, occasionally hards
- Practice 30-50 problems before senior interviews
- Focus on clear communication

#### 2. System design

New at senior. Design a system.

- "Design Playwright-like test runner"
- "Design flaky test detection service"
- "Design CI pipeline for 500-engineer org"

Resources:

- [System Design Primer](https://github.com/donnemartin/system-design-primer)
- [ByteByteGo YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@ByteByteGo)

#### 3. Test design interview (QA-specific)

- "Design test strategy for Instagram"
- "Test cases for an elevator"
- "How would you test autonomous driving?"

Show: exploration, clarifying questions, trade-offs, prioritization.

#### 4. Behavioral

STAR framework (Doc 29 in bootcamp).

- "Tell me about time you disagreed with dev..."
- "Walk me through a hard bug..."
- "How do you handle stakeholder pressure?"

Prep 10 stories covering common themes.

#### 5. Portfolio / past work

Walk through real projects.

- Tech stack choices
- Trade-offs made
- Lessons learned
- AI involvement (2026)

#### 6. Case / take-home

- Build small test suite
- Review given code
- Propose strategy

Senior take-homes often larger (2-8 hours). Manage time carefully.

### Interview day tips

#### Before

- Sleep 8 hours
- Review notes + stories
- Eat well
- Test setup (video, audio)

#### During

- Think out loud
- Ask clarifying questions (senior hallmark)
- Admit don't-knows gracefully
- Push back thoughtfully when wrong

#### After

- Send thank-you note (within 24h)
- Reflect on what went well/badly
- Ask for feedback if declined

### Avoiding senior-specific pitfalls

#### Trap 1: Over-engineering design

Questions often ambiguous — don't assume full Google scale.
Ask about scale first. Design appropriately.

#### Trap 2: Rambling stories

Behavioral: 1-2 minutes max. Edit ruthlessly.

#### Trap 3: Weak "why senior"

Prepare: "I'm senior because I do X, Y, Z. Specific examples: [case 1, case 2]."

#### Trap 4: Disparaging past companies

Never. Reframe neutral.
Instead of "Bad management" → "Different priorities than I wanted."

## 📄 Resume + portfolio for senior

### Resume rules

#### One page? Maybe two at senior.

Content over formatting.

#### Structure

```
Name | Email | LinkedIn | GitHub

Senior Automation Test Engineer | 6 yrs

## Highlights (3-5 bullets)
- Led test automation for X product (team of 15)
- Reduced flaky rate 12% → 1.5% via [specific]
- Built AI agent saving team X hours/week
- Speaker at [conference/meetup]
- Contributed to [OSS project]

## Experience
### Company 1 | Senior QA Engineer | 2024-present
- Impact bullet (scope + number)
- Impact bullet
- Impact bullet

### Company 2 | QA Engineer | 2022-2024
...

## Skills
Playwright, TypeScript, AI agents (Claude Agent SDK),
k6 (load testing), OWASP, GitHub Actions, Docker,
PostgreSQL, OpenTelemetry

## Education
[Relevant]
```

### Bullet writing formula

**Bad:**
"Worked on Playwright tests."

**Good:**
"Built Playwright test suite covering 90% of critical flows, reducing regression time from 8h manual to 15min automated."

Format: Action + scope + result + impact

### Portfolio repo

Single "hero" repo:

- Your `playwright-learning-journey` or similar
- CI green ≥ 30 days
- README stellar
- Demo GIF
- Blog post linked

Supplementary repos if specialized (AI agents, performance tests, etc.)

### LinkedIn optimization

#### Headline

"Senior QA Engineer | Playwright + AI-native testing | Open to senior roles"

#### About (3 paragraphs)

1. What you do (senior tester, your specialty)
2. Unique angle (AI? performance? mobile?)
3. What you're looking for

#### Featured

Pin: portfolio repo + blog + talk video

#### Skills + endorsements

Top 10 relevant. Get ex-colleagues to endorse.

### Blog (important for senior)

Expected: 1-2 posts/month.

Topics that work:

- Deep dives (not beginner content)
- Lessons from real experience
- Opinions (have them)
- Tutorials for intermediate/advanced

Platform: dev.to, personal site, Medium.

## 🗺️ Where to apply (senior)

### Company tiers (by compensation + prestige)

#### Tier 1: Big Tech (hardest, highest pay)

Google, Meta, Netflix, Apple, Microsoft, Amazon, etc.

- Rigorous interviews (5-8 rounds)
- Leetcode-heavy
- High pay ($150-300K+ junior-senior)

#### Tier 2: Late-stage startups (mid-difficulty, variable pay + equity)

Stripe, Airbnb, Notion, Figma, etc.

- Cultural fit huge
- Equity potentially lucrative
- Interview quality varies

#### Tier 3: Mid-stage startups (varies)

Series B-C, 100-500 employees.

- Move fast, impact high
- Pay moderate, equity variable
- Fewer interview rounds

#### Tier 4: Enterprise / traditional

Banks, insurance, telcos.

- Process-heavy
- Stable, good for work-life
- Compensation OK, not tech-top

#### Tier 5: Bootstrapped / agencies

Service companies.

- Variety of clients
- Moderate pay
- Good learning early career

**Local companies:**

- Topica, VNPay, Momo, Tiki, VinAI
- Reputable, competitive pay for local market

**Multinationals in Vietnam:**

- Agoda, Klook, Axon, ELSA Speak
- Pay 1.5-2x local

**Remote global:**

- GitLab, Automattic, Shopify
- USD comp but US interview standards

**Big Tech:**

- Very competitive, but possible
- Often via regional office (Singapore, Tokyo)

## 🎯 30-60-90 day plan (new senior role)

### First 30 days: Learn

- Understand systems (tech + people)
- Meet everyone important
- Read docs, code, history
- Don't propose changes yet
- Ask many questions

### 30-60: Contribute

- Fix 2-3 concrete things
- Add 1 process improvement
- Ship small but visible

### 60-90: Lead

- Own 1 project / initiative
- Propose 1 medium-size change
- Formally mentor someone

### Document in 1-page plan, share with manager.

## 📈 Continuous growth (after landing role)

### Quarterly review (self)

Each quarter, reflect:

- What did I ship?
- What impact beyond my team?
- What did I learn (new skill)?
- Who did I help?
- Where am I stuck?

Adjust next quarter accordingly.

### Annual promotion criteria

Before thinking promotion:

- Exceeded expectations 2-4 quarters
- Operating at next level already (not just doing current)
- Manager sees it
- Leadership knows you

If not ready → ask manager specifically: "What would make me ready for [next level] in 6-12 months?"

### When to leave

Signals:

- Growth stopped (1-2 years plateau)
- Manager can't advocate for you
- Comp below market >10%
- Company health declining
- Team toxicity

If staying: optimize visibility, broaden scope.
If leaving: planned search (3-6 months), don't leave reactively.
